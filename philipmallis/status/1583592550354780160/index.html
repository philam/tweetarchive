So a number of nearby streets have names associated with the Royal Navy. Victory Street is named for HMS Victory (among other things, Admiral Nelson's flagship during the Battle of Trafalgar). This is where Terror Street comes in.

The name HMS Terror has been used for several Royal Navy ships. One of the most famous was built in 1813 & sailed on polar exploration missions. Its last one, led by Sir John Franklin, ended in disaster. HMS Terror sank in 1845 & was not found until 2016 https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franklin%27s_lost_expedition

The ship it sailed with, HMS Erebus, was also lost on the same trip. Hence we have Terror Street and Erebus Street next to each other. An added clue is Francis Street, which may be named for the HMS Terror's commander, Francis Crozier. https://www.openstreetmap.org/#map=17/-37.72617/144.86142
